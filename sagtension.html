Overhead Conductor Sag-Tension

Method & Assumptions

This study computes the sag and tension of an overhead conductor across a line section using the ruling-span method, NESC loading districts, and the elastic/thermal change-of-state equation. It produces a contractor stringing table for the bare conductor over a temperature range.

Geometry:

Ruling span  RS = √(Σ Sᵢ³ / Σ Sᵢ)
Parabolic sag D = w·S² / (8·H)
Catenary sag  D = (H/w)·(cosh(w·S / 2H) − 1)

NESC loading (IEEE C2 §250): radial ice (57 lb/ft³) plus transverse wind pressure combine into a resultant unit load, with a district constant added: Heavy (0.50 in ice, 4 psf, K=0.30, 0 °F), Medium (0.25 in, 4 psf, K=0.20, 15 °F), Light (0 in, 9 psf, K=0.05, 30 °F).

Change of state relates the horizontal tension at two conditions:

H₂²·[H₂ − H₁ + αEA(t₂−t₁) + EA·w₁²S²/24H₁²] = EA·w₂²S²/24

The design (limiting) tension is set as a percentage of the conductor's rated strength (UTS) at the selected loading district, and the stringing table is back-calculated for the bare conductor at each installation temperature.

References: IEEE Std 524; NESC (IEEE C2) §250; ASCE Manual 74.

Conductor Sag-Tension Help

This study sizes the sag and tension of overhead conductors for substation yard tie-ins, distribution getaways, and short transmission sections.

When to use

  • Substation yard bus and shield-wire stringing.
  • Overhead distribution getaways and yard lighting tie-ins.
  • Ground-clearance checks under maximum-sag (hot or iced) conditions.
  • Producing a contractor stringing table at the day's installation temperature.

Inputs

  • Conductor — select an ACSR/AAC/AAAC from the library; values are representative and should be confirmed against the datasheet.
  • Spans — list every span in the section; the ruling span (the tension-governing equivalent span) is computed for you.
  • NESC district — the ice/wind/temperature loading case used to set the limiting tension.
  • Design tension — the maximum tension as a percentage of rated strength (UTS).

Interpreting results

  • Design sag — the sag at the loaded design condition; use it for clearance checks.
  • Loading cases — tension and sag at each NESC district, with the percentage of UTS used.
  • Stringing table — the bare-conductor horizontal tension and sag at each installation temperature, for the line crew.

Limitations

  • Level spans and final-modulus behaviour (no separate initial/creep curve).
  • No galloping, aeolian vibration, or inclined-span correction.
  • Screening-level — verify against a manufacturer sag-tension chart before construction.
