Why CableTrayRoute?

CableTrayRoute streamlines electrical design by linking scheduling, analysis, and routing tools in one browser-based workflow. Engineers can define cables, assess tray and conduit capacity, and generate optimal paths without juggling spreadsheets. Automated checks against fill limits and thermal constraints reduce errors and rework. The unified interface accelerates planning and helps deliver consistent, code-compliant results on complex projects.

CableTrayRoute Help

Browser-based toolkit for cable scheduling, analysis, and optimal routing. Work through the steps in order — data flows automatically from one tool to the next.

7-Step Workflow

  1. Cable Schedule Define cables: tag, size, conductor type, allowed raceway groups, and start/end coordinates.
  2. Raceway Schedule Catalog trays, conduits, and ductbanks — including dimensions, fill limits, and node coordinates.
  3. Ductbank Analysis Analyze underground ductbanks for thermal constraints and cable placement.
  4. Tray Fill Visualize cross-sectional cable placement in trays and check fill percentages.
  5. Conduit Fill Evaluate standalone conduit loading against NEC fill limits.
  6. Optimal Cable Route Run Dijkstra-based pathfinding to find least-cost routes respecting tray capacity and thermal limits. View results in 3D.
  7. One-Line Diagram Draw and edit interactive electrical single-line diagrams, then export to PDF or DXF.

Tips

  • Projects auto-save to your browser. Use Save Project to sync to the server or Export Project to download a .ctr.json backup.
  • The Progress Overview on this page tracks which steps have data and shows your next recommended action.
  • Most tables support CSV import and export — look for the import/export toolbar on each page.
