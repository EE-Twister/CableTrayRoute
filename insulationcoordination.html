Insulation Coordination

IEC 60071-1/2 · IEEE 1313 — BIL/SIL selection, arrester protective margins, statistical risk of failure

Method & Assumptions

Insulation coordination selects the lowest standard insulation level (BIL and SIL from IEC 60071-1 Tables 2 and 3) that satisfies the required coordination withstand voltage at the equipment location, accounting for altitude, safety factor, and surge arrester protective levels.

Step 1 — Required coordination withstand voltage (IEC 60071-2 §3):

Ucw = Urp × Ks × Ka

where Urp = representative overvoltage at the equipment (kV peak), Ks = safety factor (1.15 deterministic / 1.05 statistical), Ka = atmospheric correction factor.

Step 2 — Atmospheric correction (IEC 60071-2 §3.3):

Ka = e^(m × H / 8150)

H = altitude (m), m = 1.0 for lightning impulse self-restoring, 0.75 for power frequency non-self-restoring.

Step 3 — Standard BIL selection: Lowest standard LIWV ≥ Ucw from IEC 60071-1 Table 2 (Range I: Um ≤ 245 kV) or Table 3 (Range II: Um > 245 kV).

Step 4 — Surge arrester protective margin (IEEE 1313.2 §6):

Mp (%) = (Ucw / Ures − 1) × 100

Required: Mp ≥ 20% for lightning impulse, Mp ≥ 15% for switching impulse.

Step 5 — Statistical risk of failure (IEC 60071-2 Annex A, optional): Simplified Gaussian convolution of the overvoltage stress distribution against the insulation withstand characteristic. Target: R ≤ 10⁻⁴ per event (transmission) or 10⁻³ (distribution).

System Data

Optional descriptive label for the study.

kV

System nominal voltage (line-to-line rms). Used for display and TOV calculation reference.

Select Um from IEC 60071-1. For 138 kV systems use Um = 145 kV; for 230 kV systems use Um = 245 kV.

m

Altitude correction increases withstand requirements. At sea level Ka = 1.0. At 1000 m Ka ≈ 1.13 (LI).

Determines the temporary overvoltage (TOV) magnitude and the minimum surge arrester MCOV requirement.

Deterministic approach uses a higher safety factor. Statistical approach uses Ks = 1.05 and enables risk-of-failure estimation when stress distribution parameters are entered.

Surge Arrester
kV rms

Maximum Continuous Operating Voltage of the surge arrester. For solidly earthed 145 kV systems: MCOV ≥ 145/√3 ≈ 83.7 kV. Leave blank to skip MCOV check.

Lightning Impulse Stress
kV peak

Lightning impulse representative overvoltage at the equipment terminals. Typically taken as the arrester lightning impulse residual voltage (protective level) Ures_LI at the maximum discharge current (e.g. 10 kA for station class).

kV peak

Surge arrester lightning impulse protective level at 10 kA (station class) or 5 kA (intermediate class). From manufacturer data sheet. Required to compute protective margin Mp ≥ 20%.

Switching Impulse Stress (Range II or large systems)
kV peak

Switching impulse representative overvoltage at the equipment. Typically the arrester switching impulse residual voltage, or 2.0–2.5 pu of Um peak for unprotected systems.

kV peak

Surge arrester switching impulse protective level. From manufacturer data sheet. Used to compute Mp ≥ 15%.

Insulation Coordination Help

Insulation coordination selects equipment withstand voltages that are economically and technically justified given the overvoltage stresses and the protection provided by surge arresters.

When to use

  • Selecting transformer BIL for a new substation.
  • Verifying that the selected surge arrester provides adequate protection margin for HV cables, GIS, or power transformers.
  • Altitude correction for high-elevation substations (> 1000 m above sea level).
  • Statistical risk assessment for transmission projects requiring NERC or utility-level reliability targets.

Standard insulation levels (IEC 60071-1)

  • Range I (Um ≤ 245 kV): BIL (LIWV) and PFWV selected from Table 2.
  • Range II (Um > 245 kV): BIL and SIL (SIWV) selected from Table 3. Switching impulse governs.

Protective margin requirements (IEEE 1313.2 §6)

  • Lightning impulse: Mp ≥ 20% between equipment BIL and arrester LI protective level.
  • Switching impulse: Mp ≥ 15% between equipment SIL and arrester SI protective level.

Typical BIL values by voltage class

  • 34.5 kV (Um = 36 kV): 170 kV BIL
  • 69 kV (Um = 72.5 kV): 325 kV BIL
  • 115 kV (Um = 123 kV): 550 kV BIL
  • 138 kV (Um = 145 kV): 650 kV BIL
  • 230 kV (Um = 245 kV): 1050 kV BIL
  • 345 kV (Um = 362 kV): 1175 kV BIL (Range II)
  • 500 kV (Um = 550 kV): 1550 kV BIL (Range II)
