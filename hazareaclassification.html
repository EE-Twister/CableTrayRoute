Hazardous Area Classification

Define classified areas (NEC 500–505 Class/Division or IEC 60079 Zone), assign Ex-rated equipment, and verify protection-type and T-class compatibility per NEC Article 500 and IEC 60079-0.

Method & Standards

Classification standards: Two parallel systems are supported:

  • NEC (Articles 500–506): Class I/II/III, Division 1/2, with gas groups A–D (Class I) and dust groups E–G (Class II). Class I, Division 1 corresponds approximately to IEC Zones 0 + 1; Division 2 corresponds to Zone 2.
  • IEC 60079-10-1 / NEC Article 505: Zone 0/1/2 for flammable gases/vapours; Zone 20/21/22 (IEC 60079-10-2 / NEC Article 506) for combustible dust. Equipment groups IIA/IIB/IIC cover gas severity; IIIA/IIIB/IIIC cover dust severity.

Equipment compatibility checks (IEC 60079-0): Three rules are verified for each device in a classified area:

  1. Protection type vs. zone: Ex d (flameproof) and Ex ia (intrinsic safety Cat ia) are rated for Zone 0; Ex e (increased safety) requires Zone 1 or 2; Ex n/nA are Zone 2 only. Dust protection types (Ex t, Ex tb) apply to dust zones only.
  2. Equipment group vs. gas/dust group: An equipment group must cover the area's hazard severity. Group IIC covers IIB and IIA; Group IIIC covers IIIB and IIIA.
  3. T-rating (maximum surface temperature): Equipment T-rating must be ≤ the area's required T-class (T6 = 85 °C is the most restrictive; T1 = 450 °C is least restrictive).

Note: This tool provides a compatibility screening check. A formal area classification study per API RP 505 or IEC 60079-10-1 and the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ) approval are required before installation.

Classified Areas

Define each hazardous area. Assign a unique ID (used to link equipment). Choose NEC Class/Division or IEC Zone classification.

Ex-Rated Equipment

Enter each device installed in a classified area. Assign it to an Area ID defined on the Classified Areas tab. The checker verifies Ex protection type, equipment group, and T-rating against the area's requirements.

Hazardous Area Classification Help

This study defines classified hazardous areas and checks Ex-rated equipment compatibility per NEC Articles 500–506 and IEC 60079-0.

Classified Areas Tab

  • Standard: Choose NEC (Class/Division) or IEC (Zone) to match your project's code of record.
  • Area ID: Short unique identifier (e.g. "pump-room", "compressor-shed"). Used to link equipment to an area.
  • Class / Division (NEC): Class I = flammable gas; Class II = combustible dust; Class III = ignitable fibres. Division 1 = hazardous under normal conditions; Division 2 = abnormal conditions only.
  • Zone (IEC): Zone 0 = continuous; Zone 1 = likely (normal); Zone 2 = unlikely (abnormal). Dust zones: 20/21/22.
  • Gas / Dust Group: Identifies the specific hazardous substance class. Equipment must be rated for the group.
  • T-Rating: Minimum T-class required by the area (based on the substance's auto-ignition temperature). T6 = 85 °C (most restrictive); T1 = 450 °C (least restrictive).

Equipment Checker Tab

  • Area ID: Must match an Area ID defined on the Classified Areas tab.
  • Ex Protection Type: IEC 60079 protection method (d = flameproof, e = increased safety, ia/ib = intrinsic safety, t = dust enclosure, etc.).
  • Equipment Group: IEC group stamped on the nameplate (IIA/IIB/IIC for gas; IIIA/IIIB/IIIC for dust).
  • T-Rating: Maximum surface temperature class stamped on the nameplate (T1–T6). Must be ≤ area requirement.
  • Cert Number: ATEX, IECEx, or NEC listing certificate reference for traceability.

Result Codes

  • PASS: All checks satisfied — equipment is suitable for the assigned area.
  • FAIL: One or more compatibility failures — do not install without resolution.
  • WARN: Missing data (no cert number, no T-rating declared) — verify before installation.
