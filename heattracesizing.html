Method & Assumptions

This page follows a simplified steady-state heat-loss method used for electric heat tracing studies. Inputs are validated, cylindrical insulation resistance is calculated, and environmental film effects are applied before selecting a practical cable output (W/ft).

Q' = ΔT / (R_insulation + R_external)
Q'final = Q' × materialFactor × (1 + designMargin)
selectedWperFt = nearest standard cable rating ≥ required W/ft

The model is intended for screening-level sizing. Final selections should be checked against manufacturer circuit-length limits, startup requirements, and control strategy.

          Heat Loss Detail

          Heat loss is the thermal load: how much energy leaves the pipe through insulation, external air, or buried soil. It drives the required heat-trace watt density.

          Heat Trace Branch Circuit Review

          This sizes the heat-trace branch circuit from the heat-trace controller or panel to the traced run. It is not the upstream facility feeder; use this load when coordinating breaker, branch wiring, and panel capacity upstream.

          Temperature Profile Detail

          Temperature profile is the expected pipe temperature along the run after applying the selected heat-trace output. Heat Loss explains the load to overcome; Temperature Profile explains whether the maintained pipe temperature stays above the target along the circuit.
