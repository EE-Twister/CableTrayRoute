Method & Standards

This tool implements inverter-based resource (IBR) modeling per IEEE 1547-2018 (Interconnection and Interoperability of Distributed Energy Resources) and IEEE 2800-2022 (IBR Interconnection with Transmission Systems). Calculations cover:

1. PV Array Output (IEC 61727 / IEC 60891):

PDC = PSTC × (G / GSTC) × [1 + γP × (T − 25)]
PAC = PDC × ηinv   (clamped to Srated if curtailed)

where GSTC = 1000 W/m², T is cell temperature (°C), γP is the power temperature coefficient (%/°C), and ηinv is inverter efficiency.

2. P-Q Capability & Volt-VAR Droop (IEEE 1547-2018 Table 8):

Qmax = √(Srated² − Pout²)   [kvar]
Qdroop = interp(Vbus, Volt–VAR curve, category A or B)

3. Current-Limited Fault Contribution (IEEE 1547-2018 §6.4 / IEEE 2800-2022 §6.7.1):

Irated = Srated / (√3 × VLL)
Ifault = klimit × Irated   (klimit = 1.05–1.2, default 1.1)

Inverters do not contribute synchronous machine-level fault currents; the fault current is limited by the inverter control loop to a small multiple of rated current.

4. BESS Dispatch:

PAC,discharge = Psetpoint × ηrt   (blocked if SOC ≤ SOCmin)
PAC,charge = −Psetpoint / ηrt   (blocked if SOC ≥ SOCmax)

5. Frequency-Watt Response (IEEE 1547-2018 §5.3.1):

fdev = f − fdeadband,high   (when f > fdb,high)
curtail = min(1, droop% × fdev)
Pdispatch = Pmax × (1 − curtail)

References: IBR Modeling Guide. See also: Load Flow, Short Circuit, Battery / UPS Sizing.

PV Array Parameters

Nameplate DC power rating of the array at Standard Test Conditions (1000 W/m², 25 °C). Sum of all module ratings in kW.

Irradiance on the module surface. STC = 1000 W/m². Typical peak = 900–1100 W/m². Annual average varies by location (450–700 W/m² for US sites).

Module cell temperature (not ambient). Estimated as Tamb + (G / 800) × (NOCT − 20). Typical NOCT = 44 °C. STC = 25 °C.

Power loss per °C above STC (25 °C). Typical mono-Si: −0.35 to −0.40 %/°C. Thin-film (CdTe): −0.19 to −0.25 %/°C.

CEC-weighted or peak efficiency. Modern string inverters: 96–98.5 %. Central inverters: 97–98 %. Microinverters: 95–96.5 %.

Inverter apparent power limit. DC:AC ratio = PSTC / Srated. Typical ratio: 1.0–1.3. When PAC exceeds this limit, output is curtailed (clipped).

IBR Fault Contribution Parameters

Total inverter apparent power at the point of common coupling.

Line-to-line voltage at the inverter terminals. Common: 0.48 kV, 4.16 kV, 12.47 kV, 34.5 kV.

Ratio of fault current to rated current. IEEE 1547-2018 §6.4 default: 1.05–1.2. IEEE 2800-2022 (transmission): up to 1.2. Grid-forming inverters may reach 1.5.

When unchecked, the inverter trips off immediately and contributes zero fault current (default IEEE 1547-2018 Category I behavior).
