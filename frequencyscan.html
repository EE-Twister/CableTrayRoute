Harmonic Frequency Scan

Method & Assumptions

This study sweeps the Thevenin driving-point impedance Z(h) at the bus from the 1st to the 50th harmonic order (0.5-step increments). The single-bus model is:

Z_dp(h) = Z_source(h) ‖ ΣZ_cap(h) ‖ ΣZ_filter(h)

Source and cable impedances are combined in series. Capacitor banks and detuned filters are modelled as shunt elements. Reactive components scale linearly with harmonic order (X ∝ h); the source R/X ratio sets the fundamental reactance.

Parallel resonance (impedance peak) occurs where inductive and capacitive reactances cancel: h_r ≈ √(S_sc_kVA / Q_kVAR). Risk classification uses IEEE 519-2022 dominant harmonic orders (5, 7, 11, 13, …).

This model is appropriate for preliminary filter design screening and harmonic amplification assessment. Complex multi-bus systems require a full Y-bus frequency sweep.

Frequency Scan Help

This study sweeps the driving-point impedance at a bus from the 1st to the 50th harmonic. Peaks indicate parallel resonance (voltage amplification); troughs indicate series resonance (current amplification).

When to use

  • After sizing a capacitor bank — check if the resonance order falls near a dominant harmonic (5th, 7th, 11th, 13th).
  • Before specifying harmonic filters — use the resonance map to choose detuning reactor % that shifts resonance below the 5th harmonic.
  • When investigating elevated harmonic voltage levels on a bus.

Risk classification

  • Danger — parallel resonance within ±0.5 harmonic orders of a dominant harmonic (5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19, 23, 25).
  • Caution — within ±1.0 harmonic orders.
  • Low — more than 1 harmonic order away from any dominant harmonic.

Typical detuning reactor percentages

  • 5.67% — tunes to h ≈ 4.2 (protects 5th harmonic)
  • 7% — tunes to h ≈ 3.8 (protects 5th harmonic, wider margin)
  • 14% — tunes to h ≈ 2.7 (protects 3rd harmonic)
