Lightning & Surge Protection

Method & Assumptions

This study performs a screening-level lightning protection assessment per IEC 62305 (structural LPS) and IEEE 998 (substation shielding), and recommends a surge-arrester continuous operating voltage per IEEE C62.22 / IEC 60099-5.

Risk:

Ng = 0.04 · Td^1.25                  (flashes/km²/yr)
Ad = L·W + 2·(3H)(L+W) + π·(3H)²     (collection area, m²)
Nd = Ng · Ad · Cd · 1e-6             (direct strikes/yr)
E  = 1 − Nc/Nd  →  LPL I / II / III / IV

The required Lightning Protection Level (LPL) comes from the protection-efficiency table: E>0.98 → I, >0.95 → II, >0.90 → III, >0.80 → IV.

Rolling sphere (electrogeometric model): each LPL has a sphere radius R (I=20 m, II=30 m, III=45 m, IV=60 m). A single mast of height h protects an object of height hx out to a radius:

rp = √(h(2R − h)) − √(hx(2R − hx))

Surge arrester: minimum MCOV Uc ≥ 1.05·VLL/√3 (effectively grounded) or 1.05·VLL (ungrounded); rated voltage ≈ MCOV/0.8.

References: IEC 62305-1/-2/-3; IEEE Std 998; IEEE C62.22 / IEC 60099-5.

Lightning & Surge Protection Help

This study estimates lightning strike risk to a structure, recommends a Lightning Protection Level, sizes the rolling-sphere air termination and down-conductors, and selects a surge-arrester rating.

When to use

  • Utility substation direct-stroke shielding (IEEE 998).
  • Structural lightning protection for control buildings, telecom huts, and BESS enclosures (IEC 62305).
  • Surge-arrester MCOV selection for incoming line protection.

Inputs

  • Thunderstorm days — the site keraunic level from an isokeraunic map, or enter the ground flash density directly.
  • Location factor — accounts for nearby taller objects that intercept strikes.
  • Structure geometry — sets the equivalent collection area.
  • System voltage / grounding — drives the surge-arrester MCOV.

Interpreting results

  • Required LPL — the protection class that brings the residual strike frequency below the tolerable value.
  • Rolling-sphere radius and single-mast protective radius size the air termination.
  • Down-conductors — count and minimum cross-section per the class spacing.
  • Surge arrester — minimum MCOV and the nearest standard duty-cycle rating.

Limitations

  • Screening-level — a simplified single-component risk, not the full IEC 62305-2 R1–R4 assessment.
  • Single-mast protection only; multi-mast and shield-wire layouts need a dedicated study.
  • Verify against a full risk assessment before final design.
