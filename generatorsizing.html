Generator Sizing — NFPA 110 / NEC 700–702

Method & Assumptions

Generator sizing follows NFPA 110-2022 (Emergency and Standby Power Systems), NEC Articles 700–702, and IEEE 446-1995 (Recommended Practice for Emergency Power Systems). Site derating uses manufacturer-published correction curves as codified in NFPA 110 Annex B and ISO 8528-1.

Altitude derating (NFPA 110 Annex B):

altitudeFactor = 1 − 0.03 × max(0, (altitude_ft − 500) / 1000)   [naturally-aspirated]
altitudeFactor = 1 − 0.01 × max(0, (altitude_ft − 500) / 1000)   [turbocharged]

Temperature derating (ISO 8528-1, standard rating point 40 °C):

tempFactor = 1 − 0.01 × max(0, ambientC − 40)

Motor start step load (IEEE 446 §5.3):

startingKVA = (HP × 0.746) / (PF × efficiency) × LRC_multiplier

Transient voltage dip (IEEE 446 §5.4):

dip% = (stepLoadKVA / genKVA) × X'd%

NFPA 110 Type 10 systems must keep voltage dip ≤ 35% during the largest motor start.

Fuel runtime:

fuelRate (gal/hr) = loadKW × 1.341 × SFC_lb_per_hp_hr / 6.791
runtime (hr) = tankCapacity_gal / fuelRate

Default SFC = 0.38 lb/hp-hr (diesel, ~75% load). Diesel density = 6.791 lb/US gal at 60 °F.

Site Conditions

Installation altitude above mean sea level. Derating begins above 500 ft. Generator output decreases by 3% per 1,000 ft (naturally-aspirated) or 1% per 1,000 ft (turbocharged) above 500 ft per NFPA 110 Annex B.

Maximum ambient temperature at the generator installation location. Standard rating point is 40 °C (ISO 8528-1). Output is derated 1% per °C above 40 °C. Use the highest expected summer temperature for conservative sizing.

Turbocharged engines maintain output better at altitude. Most modern generators above 150 kW are turbocharged; smaller portable units are often naturally-aspirated. Confirm with the manufacturer's data sheet.

The NFPA 110 type determines the maximum allowable transfer time and minimum runtime requirements. Type 10 is required for hospital essential electrical systems, egress lighting, and fire protection. Consult the AHJ for classification.

Largest Motor Step Load (Optional)

Enter the largest motor connected to the generator. The motor starting current creates a step kVA demand that may size the generator larger than the continuous load alone. Leave HP at 0 to skip this check. See the Motor Start study for detailed starting analysis.

Nameplate HP of the largest motor that may start across-the-line while on generator power. For soft-start or VFD-driven motors, use a lower LRC multiplier (1.0–1.5×) below.

Locked-rotor current as a multiple of full-load amps. Typical values: across-the-line start 5–7×; star-delta start 2–3×; soft starter 2–4×; VFD 1–1.5×. Use NEMA Design B default of 6× if no nameplate data is available.

X'd governs the initial voltage dip during a motor start or sudden step load. Typical range: 20–30% for synchronous generators. A lower X'd means a stiffer generator (smaller voltage dip). Obtain from the generator data sheet.

Fuel & Runtime

Usable capacity of the base tank or day tank. Leave at 0 to skip the fuel runtime calculation. NFPA 110 §8.3.1 requires a minimum of 2 hours of fuel for Type 10 systems (many AHJs require 4–8 hours; mission-critical facilities often specify 24–72 hours).

Diesel SFC at approximately 75% load. Typical range: 0.35–0.45 lb/hp-hr. Use the generator data sheet value for the expected operating load. Default 0.38 lb/hp-hr is a conservative estimate for modern Tier 4 diesel engines.

Generator Sizing Help

This tool sizes a standby or emergency generator per NFPA 110-2022 and NEC Articles 700–702.

Workflow

  1. Enter all loads that must be served by the generator in the Load Schedule table. Obtain load kW from nameplates or the Load Flow study.
  2. Enter the site altitude and design ambient temperature. The tool applies NFPA 110 Annex B derating automatically.
  3. Enter the largest motor HP and LRC multiplier to check the motor start voltage dip. For VFD-driven motors, use a low LRC multiplier (1.0–1.5×).
  4. Enter the fuel tank capacity to calculate estimated runtime.
  5. Click Run Analysis to see the recommended generator size.

Standards
