Differential Protection (87B / 87T / 87G)

Method & Assumptions

Models the percentage-differential characteristic used by modern numerical differential relays (SEL-387/487B, GE T60, ABB RET670). Based on IEEE C37.91-2008 (transformer), IEEE C37.102-2006 (generator), and IEEE C37.97-1979 (bus).

Operating and restraint currents:

I_op  = | I₁/n₁  −  I₂/n₂ |          (differential current)
I_rst = ( |I₁/n₁| + |I₂/n₂| ) / 2    (restraint current)

Dual-slope trip threshold:

Slope 1 region  (I_rst < I_bp):
  threshold = max(I_min, S₁ × I_rst)
Slope 2 region  (I_rst ≥ I_bp):
  threshold = S₁×I_bp + S₂×(I_rst − I_bp)

Trip if I_op > threshold AND harmonic restraint is not active.

Harmonic restraint (IEEE C37.91 §8.2):

87T: 2nd harmonic ≥ 15% → restrain (inrush blocking)
87T/87G: 5th harmonic ≥ 35% → restrain (over-excitation blocking)

Citations: Differential Protection Guide. See also: TCC Coordination, Short Circuit, Arc Flash.

Zone Identification

Descriptive label used in the results report only.

87T is the most common. 87B is used for bus protection. 87G protects generators. Harmonic restraint prevents false trips during transformer inrush (2nd harmonic) and over-excitation (5th harmonic).

Current Transformer (CT) Ratios

Primary turns of CT on terminal 1 (high-voltage side of transformer, or line 1 of bus). Enter the numerator of the ratio — e.g., enter 600 for a 600:5 CT.

Primary turns of CT on terminal 2 (low-voltage side of transformer, or line 2 of bus).

Dimensionless relay compensation tap. Nominal value = CT₁ / CT₂ (e.g., 600 / 100 = 6). Set to the nearest available relay setting. Mismatch > 5% triggers a warning per IEEE C37.91.

Relay Characteristic Settings

Through-fault restraint slope for low currents. Typical: 25%. SEL relays commonly use 25% for Slope 1.

High-current restraint slope (CT saturation region). Must be > Slope 1. Typical: 65%.

Floor of the operate region. The relay will not trip below this differential current regardless of restraint. Typical: 0.20 pu. Per-unit of CT secondary / tap setting.

Restraint current (pu) at which the slope transitions from S₁ to S₂. Typically set just above full-load through current to avoid CT saturation effects during external faults. Typical: 3.0 pu.

Operating Currents

Current flowing into the protected zone at terminal 1 (A, primary side). Use the fault study or load flow result for the condition being evaluated.

Current flowing into the zone at terminal 2. For normal through-current, this is negative (current exits the zone). For an in-zone fault, both currents flow in and should have the same sign.

Harmonic Content (for Restraint Check)

Present during transformer inrush. If ≥ 15%, the 87T relay blocks tripping (IEEE C37.91). Measure with a power quality analyser or estimate from inrush studies.

Present during transformer over-excitation. If ≥ 35%, the 87T/87G relay blocks tripping (IEEE C37.91). Enter the 5th harmonic percentage of the operating current.
