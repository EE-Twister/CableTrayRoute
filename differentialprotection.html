Differential Protection Zone Modeling (87B / 87T / 87G)

Method & Assumptions

Percentage-differential protection operates on the principle that the algebraic sum of currents entering and leaving a protected zone should be zero during normal conditions or external faults. An internal fault creates an operating current I_diff = |I₁ − I₂| that exceeds the percentage of the restraint current I_rest = (|I₁| + |I₂|) / 2.

Dual-slope characteristic (IEEE C37.91 / C37.97 / C37.101):

Region 1 (I_rest ≤ I_bp): threshold = max(Slope₁ × I_rest, I_min)
Region 2 (I_rest > I_bp): threshold = threshold(I_bp) + Slope₂ × (I_rest − I_bp)

Currents are expressed in per-unit of rated current. The relay OPERATEs (trips) when I_diff ≥ threshold.

CT ratio mismatch (IEEE C37.91 §5.3): the tap factor tap = (N_turns × CTsec) / CTpri should be ≈ 1.0. Mismatches > 10% require numerical relay tap compensation or re-selection of CTs.

Harmonic restraint (87T only) — IEEE C37.91 §6.5:

  • 2nd harmonic blocking: magnetizing inrush current contains high 2nd harmonic (typically 15–60% of fundamental). Block relay if I₂ₙd / I_diff ≥ threshold (default 20%).
  • 5th harmonic restraint: overexcitation (transformer overfluxing) produces high 5th harmonic. Restrain if I₅ₜₕ / I_diff ≥ threshold (default 35%).

References: IEEE C37.91-2008 (transformer), IEEE C37.97-2012 (bus), IEEE C37.101-2006 (generator), NERC PRC-001-2.
