Voltage Drop Compliance Study

Method & Standards

Voltage drop is calculated using the DC resistance method adjusted for AC impedance and temperature correction per conductor material:

VD% = (√3 × I × R × L) / V × 100   [3-phase]
VD% = (2 × I × R × L) / V × 100   [single-phase]

where I = load current (A), R = conductor resistance per unit length (Ω/m at operating temperature), L = one-way length (m), and V = nominal line voltage (V).

NEC limits (informational notes):

  • NEC 215.2(A)(3): Feeder circuits — ≤ 3 % recommended
  • NEC 210.19(A)(1): Branch circuits — ≤ 3 % recommended
  • Combined feeder + branch — ≤ 5 % recommended

Cables without length, load current, or voltage data are excluded from compliance checking but are listed in the table. Circuit type (feeder/branch) is determined from the Circuit Type or Service Type field in the cable schedule.

Voltage Drop Study Help

Click Run Study to analyse all cables in the current project against NEC voltage drop limits. Cables are retrieved from the Cable Schedule.

Status indicators:

  • PASS — drop is below 80 % of the NEC recommended limit
  • WARN — drop is between 80 % and 100 % of the limit (near-limit)
  • FAIL — drop exceeds the NEC recommended limit

Rows without sufficient data (missing length, current, or voltage) show a dash and are counted as PASS.
