Voltage Stability Analysis

Screening only. This sequential Newton-Raphson sweep does not implement continuation power flow and cannot establish a physical P-V/Q-V nose, voltage-collapse point, or compliance margin.
Method & Standards

This study sweeps the total system load from the operating point up to a user-specified maximum (the load scaling factor λ) and solves a Newton-Raphson power flow at each step. The last converged point is reported as a sampled numerical boundary only.

The companion Q-V sensitivity curve sweeps reactive power injection on a selected bus from negative (absorbing) to positive (injecting) and records the resulting voltage. Capacitive injection is allowed to exceed the original reactive load; no artificial zero-load clamp is applied.

Standards: NERC TPL-001-5 (Transmission Planning Performance Requirements), IEEE Std 2800-2022 (voltage stability screening), WECC Voltage Stability Methodology.

Algorithm: Sequential Newton-Raphson power flows with fixed load steps. First nonconvergence is labeled as a solver boundary—not as physical collapse. Production-grade margins require continuation power flow and complete equipment controls/limits.

Bus model: Slack (swing) bus holds voltage magnitude and angle; PV buses hold voltage magnitude and active injection; PQ buses hold active and reactive load.

System Base

Common values: 100 MVA (transmission), 10 MVA (industrial). All per-unit calculations use this base.

Buses

Define at least one slack bus and one PQ load bus. Base kV applies to that bus's per-unit conversion.

Branches

Enter series impedance in ohms. Each branch is added once; the admittance is reflected at both endpoints automatically.

P-V Curve Parameters

The sweep continues until the power flow diverges or λ reaches this value. A value of 3.0 means 300% of base load.

Smaller steps give a smoother sensitivity sweep but do not convert solver nonconvergence into a physical nose-point calculation.

Q-V Curve Parameters

The bus at which reactive injection is swept. Leave blank to use the first PQ bus automatically.

Voltage Stability Help

P-V Curve (Nose Curve)

The P-V sweep plots voltage at each bus as total system load is scaled from the operating point. Newton-Raphson divergence can occur for numerical reasons and is not, by itself, evidence of voltage collapse.

Q-V Curve

The Q-V curve shows voltage sensitivity to reactive injection. This screen does not assign a reactive margin or nose point.

NERC TPL-001 Screening

NERC TPL-001-5 requires planning-event voltage stability analysis for Category P5–P7 contingencies. A commonly used screening threshold is a loadability margin ≥ 5% of operating load.

Bus Types

  • Slack: Reference bus. Voltage magnitude and angle are fixed (typically 1.0 pu, 0°). Supplies whatever power is needed to balance the system.
  • PQ: Load bus. Active (P) and reactive (Q) load are specified; voltage floats.
  • PV: Generator bus. Active injection (P) and voltage magnitude are specified; reactive output floats within limits.
