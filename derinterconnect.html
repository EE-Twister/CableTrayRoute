IEEE 1547-2018 DER Interconnection Study

Method & Standards

This tool implements the six mandatory screening criteria required by IEEE 1547-2018 (as amended by IEEE 1547a-2020) for US utility distribution interconnection of distributed energy resources (DER). Utilities require these criteria to be satisfied before issuing an interconnection agreement.

1. PCC Voltage Impact (ANSI C84.1-2020):

ΔV ≈ (P·R + Q·X) / V²   [pu]
Range A: 0.95 ≤ V ≤ 1.05 pu (normal sustained)
Range B: 0.917 ≤ V ≤ 1.083 pu (infrequent)

2. Fault Current Contribution (IEEE 1547-2018 §6.4):

Irated = Srated / (√3 × VLL)
IIBR,fault = klimit × Irated   (klimit = 1.05–1.2)
Itotal = Igrid + IIBR,fault   ≤ device interrupting rating

3. Anti-Islanding (IEEE 1547-2018 §8.1):

Category A: trip within 2.0 s
Category B: trip within 1.0 s
Category C: trip within 0.16 s

4. Voltage / Frequency Ride-Through (IEEE 1547-2018 Tables 3 & 5):

Category I:   V: 0.70–1.10 pu, f: 58.5–61.5 Hz (60 Hz system)
Category II:  V: 0.65–1.10 pu, f: 57.0–62.0 Hz
Category III: V: 0.50–1.20 pu, f: 56.5–63.0 Hz

5. Harmonic Compliance (IEEE 1547-2018 Table 2):

THD ≤ 5% of rated current
Odd harmonics (3rd–9th): 0.5–3.0% individually

References: DER Interconnection Guide. See also: IBR Modeling (PV/BESS), Load Flow, Short Circuit, Harmonics.

1. DER Configuration

Nameplate rated active power output at the point of common coupling (PCC).

Inverter AC apparent power rating. Used to calculate rated current and fault contribution.

Reactive power at rated conditions. Positive = capacitive injection (raises voltage). Leave 0 for unity power factor operation.

IEEE 1547-2018 §1.4 categories. Category B applies to resources > 500 kW or utility-specified. Category C is for DER with synchronous machine-level fault contribution.

Ride-through capability category per IEEE 1547-2018 §6.5. Category I is the default for rooftop and distribution-connected resources.

2. System Data at PCC

Line-to-line voltage at the point of common coupling. Common: 0.48 kV, 4.16 kV, 12.47 kV, 25 kV, 34.5 kV.

Steady-state voltage at the PCC before connecting the DER. From a load flow study or field measurement. Typical: 0.97–1.03 pu.

Three-phase fault MVA at the PCC (grid stiffness indicator). Obtained from the utility interconnection data or short-circuit study. Typical distribution: 50–500 MVA.

Source resistance looking from the PCC toward the utility. Determines voltage sensitivity to active power injection. Approximate from feeder impedance data.

Source reactance looking from the PCC toward the utility. Determines voltage sensitivity to reactive power injection (Volt-VAR support). Typical X/R ratio: 3–10.

Symmetrical interrupting rating of the most limiting protective device at or near the PCC (breaker, fuse, recloser). Typical distribution: 8–40 kA.

Three-phase symmetric fault current at the PCC without the DER. From a short-circuit study or utility interconnection data.

Ratio of inverter fault current to rated current. IEEE 1547-2018 §6.4: default 1.1 (1.05–1.2). IEEE 2800-2022 (transmission IBR): up to 1.2. Synchronous machine equivalent: up to 1.5.

3. Anti-Islanding Protection

Time from islanding detection to DER trip. Must be ≤ 2.0 s (Cat A), ≤ 1.0 s (Cat B), or ≤ 0.16 s (Cat C) per IEEE 1547-2018 §8.1.

Active methods provide non-detection-zone (NDZ) elimination per IEEE 1547 §8.1.3. Passive methods are acceptable but may have a non-detection zone. "None" is non-compliant.

4. Voltage / Frequency Ride-Through Settings

Enter the DER's configured trip thresholds. Compliance is verified against IEEE 1547-2018 Tables 3 & 5 for the selected ride-through category. Settings may be pre-populated from an IBR Modeling study.

5. Harmonic Current Compliance

Measured or manufacturer-specified total harmonic distortion at rated output. IEEE 1547-2018 Table 2 limit: 5%. Modern grid-tied inverters: 1–4%.

DER Interconnection Study Help

This tool evaluates the six mandatory IEEE 1547-2018 screening criteria for DER interconnection:

  1. PCC Voltage Impact — ANSI C84.1 Range A/B compliance for steady-state voltage rise.
  2. Fault Current Contribution — Total fault current vs. device interrupting capacity.
  3. Anti-Islanding — Trip time and detection method per IEEE 1547-2018 §8.1.
  4. Ride-Through — Voltage and frequency ride-through settings vs. Table 3 / Table 5.
  5. Harmonic Compliance — THD and individual harmonic current limits (Table 2).

Use Import from IBR Study to pre-populate ride-through settings from a saved IBR study.

See DER Interconnection Guide for full methodology.
