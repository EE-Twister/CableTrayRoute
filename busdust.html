Bus Duct / Cable Bus Sizing

Calculate NEC 368 derated busway ampacity, feeder voltage drop (NEC 215.2), and IEEE 605 fault-stress support span for bus duct runs.

Method & Standards

Ampacity (NEC 368): The manufacturer's rated ampacity (at 40°C ambient, horizontal installation) is derated by three independent factors applied in sequence:

FactorStandardConditions
OrientationNEC 368.12Horizontal/vertical = 1.0; edge-on = 0.80
Ambient temperatureNEC 310.15(B)(1)(a) analogy75°C-rated insulation; reference 40°C = 1.0
Stacking / proximityManufacturer guidance1 run = 1.0; 2 = 0.80; 3 = 0.70; 4+ = 0.65

Voltage drop (NEC 215.2): VD [%] = (√3 × I × L × (R·cosφ + X·sinφ) / VLL) × 100. NEC 215.2(A)(4) recommends ≤ 3% on feeder circuits.

Fault stress (IEEE 605): F/L [lbf/ft] = 0.54 × IkA² / din. Maximum span derived from Sy × Z = F × L² / 8 (simply supported beam). Allowable stress: Cu 10 000 psi, Al 6 000 psi (IEEE 605 Table 1).

Note: Ampacity and impedance data are indicative mid-range values. Verify against the selected manufacturer's published ratings before procurement.

Bus Duct Sizing Help

Enter the busway run parameters and click Calculate to obtain ampacity, voltage drop, and fault-stress results.

Conductor material: Aluminium busway typically costs less; copper has lower resistance per foot. The tool selects the smallest standard rating (800–5000 A) that meets the derated load current.

Orientation derating (NEC 368.12): Edge-on installation (conductors stacked vertically with flanges up/down) reduces the effective cooling surface and requires a 0.80 derating factor.

Voltage drop (NEC 215.2): NEC recommends ≤ 3% on feeder circuits. Long runs or high loads may require a higher-rated busway or intermediate sub-feed tap.

Fault stress (IEEE 605): The tool calculates the electromagnetic force per foot at the stated fault current and checks whether the installed support span is within the maximum allowable span for the selected busway conductor cross-section.
