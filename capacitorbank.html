Capacitor Bank Sizing & Power Factor Correction

Method & Assumptions

Power factor correction sizing follows IEEE 18-2012 (Shunt Power Capacitors) and IEEE 519-2022 (Harmonic Limits). The resonance check uses the standard parallel resonance formula.

Required capacitor kVAR (IEEE 18-2012 §7):

Q_cap = P × (tan(cos⁻¹(pf_existing)) − tan(cos⁻¹(pf_target)))

Parallel resonance harmonic order:

h_r = √(kVA_sc / kVAR_cap)

A system impedance peak (parallel resonance) occurs near harmonic h_r. If h_r coincides with a dominant harmonic produced by non-linear loads (5th, 7th, 11th, 13th), harmonic voltage amplification can damage equipment and violate IEEE 519 limits.

Detuned reactor — a series reactor placed in the capacitor branch shifts the LC resonance below the nearest harmonic. Standard detuning factors:

  • 5.67% — series resonance at h = 4.30 (protects against 5th harmonic)
  • 7% — series resonance at h = 3.78 (wider margin, protects against 5th)
  • 14% — series resonance at h = 2.68 (protects against 3rd harmonic)

Citations: Capacitor Bank Sizing Guide. See also: Harmonics, Load Flow, Short Circuit.

Capacitor Bank Sizing Help

This tool sizes a shunt capacitor bank for power factor correction (PFC) and checks for harmonic resonance risk per IEEE 18-2012 and IEEE 519-2022.

Workflow

  1. Enter the real power load (kW) and existing power factor from the Load Flow study or utility billing.
  2. Set the target power factor (typically 0.95 to avoid utility penalties).
  3. Enter the short-circuit MVA from the Short Circuit study. This enables the harmonic resonance check.
  4. Select which harmonic orders are dominant at this bus (from the Harmonics study).
  5. Click Run Analysis to see required kVAR, recommended bank size, and resonance risk.

Interpreting results

  • Required kVAR — capacitor reactive power to achieve the target PF.
  • Standard bank size — nearest NEMA standard rating ≥ required kVAR.
  • 2-stage option — two equal switched stages to avoid leading PF at light load.
  • Resonance order hr:
    • Safe — resonance is not near any dominant harmonic.
    • Caution — resonance is within 1 harmonic order of a dominant harmonic. Measure harmonic levels before energizing.
    • Danger — resonance is within 0.5 of a dominant harmonic. Install a detuned reactor before energizing.
  • Detuned reactor — series reactor in the capacitor branch that shifts resonance below the nearest dominant harmonic. The detuning factor (%) is the reactor's reactance as a percentage of the capacitor reactance at fundamental frequency.
