Prefabrication Spool Sheets

Method & Assumptions

Spool sheets group cable tray segments into prefabricated assemblies ("spools") that can be built in a workshop before field installation, reducing on-site labour by 30–50 % (NEMA VE 2 §9).

Grouping logic: Tray segments are grouped by elevation band, tray width, and plan position (grid cell). Each spool is limited to a configurable maximum number of segments to keep assemblies manageable for shop fabrication and transport.

Material quantities per spool:

  • Straight sections — Total run length ÷ section length (rounded up).
  • Support brackets — One per 10 ft span plus one at each end (minimum 2 per segment).
  • Estimated weight — Tray self-weight based on width. Does not include cable weight.
  • Cable assignments — Cables whose Route Preference matches a tray in the spool.

References: NEMA VE 2-2013 §9 (Prefabrication), BICSI TDM-1 §12, Trimble SysQue LOD-400 spool sheet methodology.

See also: Raceway Schedule, Cable Schedule, Tray Hardware BOM.

Cable Procurement Schedule

Cables are grouped by conductor specification and assigned to standard reel lengths using a first-fit decreasing algorithm. A 10% field-trim allowance is added per IEEE 1185 §6.4. Requires route results from the Optimal Route page.

Spool Sheets Help

This tool generates prefabrication spool sheets by grouping tray segments from the Raceway Schedule into workshop-fabricated assemblies.

Prerequisites:

  • Trays must have start/end X, Y, Z coordinates in the Raceway Schedule.
  • Optionally assign cables to trays via the Route Preference field in the Cable Schedule to include cable assignments in each spool.

To use:

  1. Enter trays in the Raceway Schedule.
  2. Optionally assign cables in the Cable Schedule.
  3. Adjust grouping parameters if needed.
  4. Click Generate Spool Sheets.
  5. Review each spool's materials and cable list.
  6. Click Export to Excel or Print for shop drawings.
