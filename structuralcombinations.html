Structural Load Combinations (ASCE 7-22 §2.3 / §2.4)

Method & Assumptions

This tool combines seismic and wind load effects per ASCE 7-22 to determine the governing structural demand on cable tray supports. It wraps the individual Seismic Bracing and Wind Load calculators.

Dead load D = tray self-weight + cable load (lbs/ft)

Wind lateral W per ASCE 7-22 Chapter 27/30 using velocity pressure qz = 0.00256 × Kz × Kzt × Ke × V² and force coefficient Cf (1.3 empty / 1.6 partial / 2.0 full).

Seismic lateral Eh per ASCE 7-22 §13.3.1: Fp = (0.4·ap·SDS·Wp) / (Rp/Ip) × (1 + 2z/h).

Vertical seismic effect Ev per ASCE 7-22 §12.4.2.2: Ev = 0.2·SDS·D.

LRFD combinations per §2.3.1 (gravity) and §2.3.6 (seismic):

  • LC-1: 1.4D
  • LC-2: 1.2D (L = 0 for fixed cable tray)
  • LC-3: 1.2D + 1.0W
  • LC-4: 0.9D + 1.0W (uplift check)
  • LC-5: (1.2 + 0.2·SDS)D + 1.0Eh
  • LC-6: (0.9 − 0.2·SDS)D + 1.0Eh (uplift check)

ASD combinations per §2.4.1 (gravity) and §2.4.5 (seismic).

Citations: ASCE 7-22 §2.3, §2.4, §12.4.2, §13.3.1, §13.5.6; NEMA VE 2.

Help — Structural Load Combinations

This tool evaluates ASCE 7-22 load combinations for cable tray support design, combining dead load (D), wind lateral force (W), and seismic forces (Eh, Ev).

Required inputs

  • Dead load: tray self-weight + cable load (lbs/ft)
  • Wind: wind speed (mph), exposure category, tray fill level
  • Seismic: SDS, SD1, risk category
  • Geometry: height above grade, building height, tray width, span

Result interpretation

The governing combination produces the highest demand in each direction. Compare against your support capacity (NEMA VE 1 class or manufacturer load tables) to verify adequacy.

For supports subject to both significant wind and seismic loading, the governing combination may differ by direction — a support could be controlled by wind laterally but by gravity (LC-1) vertically.
