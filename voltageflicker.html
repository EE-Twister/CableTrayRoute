Voltage Flicker Assessment

Method & Assumptions

Flicker severity is assessed using the simplified rectangular voltage-change method from IEC 61000-4-15 Annex A and IEEE 1453-2022. The method is appropriate for preliminary assessments when only the disturbance magnitude and repetition rate are known.

Step 1 — Voltage dip (IEC 61000-3-3 §4):

ΔV% = (ΔP_kW / S_sc_kVA) × 100

where S_sc_kVA is the short-circuit apparent power (kVA) at the PCC.

Step 2 — Short-term flicker severity Pst: bilinear log-log interpolation on the IEC 61000-4-15 Figure A.1 iso-Pst contours for rectangular voltage changes.

Step 3 — Long-term flicker severity Plt (IEC 61000-4-15 §4.7):

Plt = (1/12 × Σ Pst_i³)^(1/3)   over 12 consecutive 10-minute periods

Limits (IEC 61000-3-3 / IEEE 1453):

  • Pst ≤ 1.0 — mandatory limit at PCC
  • Pst ≤ 0.8 — planning level (utility allocation)

This simplified model is suitable for arc furnaces, motor starts, welders, and wind turbines with known step-change magnitudes. For continuous random fluctuations, use a full IEC 61000-4-15 signal-processing flickermeter with measured voltage waveforms.

Voltage Flicker Help

Voltage flicker is the perceptible variation in light output caused by rapid voltage fluctuations. This study computes Pst and Plt severity indices per IEC 61000-4-15 / IEEE 1453-2022.

When to use

  • Assessing arc furnace, welder, or motor start impact at the PCC before connecting to the utility.
  • Utility interconnection studies — demonstrating compliance with Pst ≤ 1.0 at the PCC.
  • Screening whether a customer's fluctuating load will cause complaints from neighbouring premises.

Limits

  • Pst ≤ 1.0 — mandatory limit at the PCC (IEC 61000-3-3 / IEEE 1453)
  • Pst ≤ 0.8 — planning level; below this value no action is normally required
  • Plt ≤ 0.8 — same planning level for long-term assessment

Typical Pst values by load type

  • Arc furnace: Pst 1–5 (requires SVC or flicker filter)
  • Resistance welder: Pst 0.5–2 (depends on repetition rate)
  • Motor start (DOL): Pst 0.2–0.8 (usually acceptable)
  • Wind turbine: Pst 0.1–0.5 (varies by tower shadow, turbulence)

Mitigation options

  • Static Var Compensator (SVC) or STATCOM — continuous reactive power control
  • Series reactor to increase source impedance ratio
  • Load scheduling — reduce simultaneous large step changes
  • Dedicated supply transformer for fluctuating loads
